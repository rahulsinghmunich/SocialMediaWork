# Design Philosophy — Bhagavad Gita Chapter 1, Shloka 1

## Movement Name
"Golden Dharma"

## Space & Geometry
The canvas breathes like a temple sanctum — wide margins, unhurried. Each slide is a single truth framed in darkness, the way a lamp illuminates one face at a time in a dimly lit sabha. The Minimal_Border layout keeps decoration at the edges only — a thin gold frame like the gilded border of an ancient manuscript. Nothing competes with the words.

## Color & Material
Deep charcoal (#212121) is not emptiness — it is the contemplative silence before Arjuna's question. Against it, gold (#FFD700) glows like a diya flame. The accent (#B8860B) is old gold, the color of aged scripture, of brass bells, of wisdom worn smooth by centuries. This is not decorative gold — it is earned, sacred, weighted.

## Typography
Italiana carries the headlines — a tall, elegant serif with the quiet authority of Sanskrit itself. It does not shout. InstrumentSans for body copy is clean and transparent, stepping aside for the meaning to come forward. For Hindi Devanagari, Hind-Bold takes the headline role — designed specifically for this script, it holds the same gravity without compromise. The NothingYouCouldDo script number sits bottom-left like a calligrapher's stroke — a human hand marking sacred sequence.

## Visual Hierarchy
The viewer's eye enters at the top label (small, low opacity — orientation only), drops to the large script number (anchor, warmth), then rises to the headline (the truth), settles into the body (the explanation), and rests on the kicker (the takeaway). The hero image — Dhritarashtra in his court — is always present beneath, always slightly veiled, like memory.

## Craftsmanship Intention
This carousel should feel like holding an illuminated page from the Gita — not a social media post. The gold frame is a hand-ruled margin. The gradient at the bottom is candlelight. Every font choice is a deliberate act of reverence. Nothing is accidental. The slides are offerings.

## Emotional Arc
Slide 1 (Hook) establishes the sacred ground — Kurukshetra, the dharma field. Slides 2–3 give the Sanskrit and its words — the original voice. Slides 4–5 bring the Hindi meaning and context — making it accessible. Slide 6 offers the deeper truth — why this moment matters. Slide 7 (CTA) is a gentle invitation, not a demand. The viewer should feel they have been in the presence of something ancient and true.
